# Jobs

Before the Great Catastrophe, most people learned their trade through a lifetime of practice and dedication, having only a few exceptions that managed to be proficient at two jobs. In the present time however, the world seems to have changed in a way that invigorated both monsters and humans alike, allowing them to obtain and master the tools of a trade without having to devote a lifetime to it. Alongside this, there's a few priests who gained the ability to immediately change the job of a person.

Once a person magically changes their job, their attributes will change to both boost the weaknesses and strengths of said job, while allowing them to retain some hard gained knowledge from their previous job, although some limits apply. 

In order to obtain jobs of higher skill and complexity, one must first master the requisite beginner jobs, and in some situations, bring some key items that enable the priest to make the change.

There's some rumours of some books showing having this same ability, however their locations are not known.

Jobs mostly determine the types of weapon you're proficient with, and what skills your mind is more keen to recall.

Due to this, a lot of adventurers make pilgrimages to the few temples that house these special priests.

# Races

Race mastery, similar to jobs, was developed and specialized throughout a lifetime before The Great Catastrophe, this is particularly relevant to monsters, as humans are not really known to evolve or have subdivisions that make much of a difference physiology wise.

After The Great Catastrophe however, monsters and humans to some degree, became able to master the limits of their bodily capabilities much faster, and with the rise of the Job Priests, Monsters became able to specialize themselves with much ease, even going so far as to have elites be able to tackle all possible versions of themselves, to then settle down to one, while keeping some knowledge and skill from their previous iterations.

To explain it so humans can understand... Think of a slime, a beginner slime will mostly be weak to magic, and be generally meh at most weapons, not really making the best use of all their potential, but showing some skill as far as using their own body goes. However, once they master this starter version of themselves, branches open up, they could focus on making sure their body composition is rejuvenating and serve as healers and helpers, others annoyed at their squishiness decide to become extremely resistant and able to absorb large amounts of damage without batting an eye, and others more devious choose a path of... dissolving their enemies to recover, turning their bodies into a deadly trap.

While a priest cannot change your race onto something completely different, like say, a human to a slime... maybe a mad scientist can?

Once a person magically changes their race, their attributes will change to both boost the weaknesses and strengths of said race, while allowing them to retain some hard gained knowledge from their previous race, although some limits apply.

Races mostly determine the types of armour you're proficient with, your elemental strengths and weaknesses, and what skills your mind is more keen to recall

In order to obtain races of higher skill and complexity, one must first master the requisite beginner races, and in some situations, bring some key items that enable the priest or scientist to make the change.
