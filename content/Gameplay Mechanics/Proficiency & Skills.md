
## Proficiency

What the fuck is proficiency? you may be asking yourself.

Well, to put it simply, is how adept you are at using certain kinds of equipment, unlike DnD, here we just have either full proficiency, or no proficiency. You can see what proficiencies you have by looking at your [[Job - Race System|Job and Race]], you can also become proficient in specific armours or weapons by equipping [[Stats#Ability Points|Abilities]].

What does having proficiency MEAN in gameplay terms? 

### For Armours

We have two perspectives to take here, RP wise.


- If a person isn't proficient with armours or otherwise "heavy" equipment (Armours, breastplates, helmets, shields of all kinds) it means that, while they WILL gain the defence, their bodies are not equipped to handle it, and thus for every point of DEF or WIL (Choosing the highest out of the two), they'll also lose a point of AGI. They will also have their Evasions of all kinds be nullified, as they cannot move with ANY easy in the heavy equipment.

- If a person isn't proficient with the lighter equipments, (magic hats, hats, fancy hats, skimpy, robes, dresses, etc.) It means that while they will gain whatever defence the piece of equipment would provide, they will not benefit from any other stat increases, as they will not be able to make the most out of it. In the case of magical items, they simply won't be able to attune to the magic inside it, and in more AGI - DEX pieces of equipment, it will just mean that despite wearing it, they cannot move any better than if they were just wearing normal clothes.

This is mostly an incentive for people to play around with classes, use Abilities, and for a wizard with 5 ATK to be able to seamlessly wield a legendary massive piece of armour, or a hardened Berserker to suddenly be filled with the magic of a god after just wearing a robe.


### For Weapons

Here's the bit that will sting,

If you try to use a weapon without being proficient in it, first of all, your Hit rate will decrease by 35% since you have a subpar control of the weapon you're wielding, making you way more likely to miss.

On top of that, since you don't have the skill to MAXIMISE the damage with the weapon you're holding, all the damage you deal will be reduced by HALF of what it would've normally been, due to clumsiness in how the weapon is handled, holding you back.

SO STICK WITH WEAPONS THAT YOU'RE GOOD WITH, USE ABILITIES AND YOUR HEAD.

## Skills

I'm not gonna leave you a list of all skills, fuck you.

I am however, gonna explain a bit more what that whole "Skill Adaptation" bit means in the [[Job - Race System|Job and Race]] things.

As you get better in a job or race, you start to develop skills that you can seamlessly pull off at the cost of some Mana or Soul Power, and that's all fine and dandy while you're still in that particular job | race.

But what happens say, if you go from a warrior to a mage? not only do you suffer form all the proficiency disparities mentioned above, but could you in theory do a double slash with your oak staff? Logically you can just swing a staff just like a sword. But the problem here lies in muscle memory and straight up memory. 

You see, when you change jobs (I'll get to races later) your body becomes adept at whatever that job entails, and that means sealing off some of that knowledge you might've acquired to focus well on the new things, particularly when they're of a different nature.
So In that previous attempt to do a double swing (A sword skill) you basically flip a coin to decide if you do it well, in the off chance of it failing, negative consequences may happen (depends on what you try exactly, but rule of thumb is, you hit yourself), since it would be like your first clumsy attempt at pulling off a refined skill. 

Now when it comes to Race based skills, things are a bit different, since, if you were a beast, there's no way you'd be able to PHYSICALLY use slime skills, right?

Well that's the fun part about obtaining race changes, your body becomes able to mutate to fullfill the needs of said skills, that's kind of what "Skill Adaptation" means in Race based skills, your body's capability of mutating to satisfy said skills.

If you "Try" to use a tentacle Skill while being a human, guess what will happen! 

Nothing, you have no tentacles, what else would happen?

So yeah, by equipping [[Stats#Ability Points|abilities]] you can enable your body's mutations to use other Race's skills, given that you've learned any of course...
