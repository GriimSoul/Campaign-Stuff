
Before delving into how you roll your starting statistics, and what are ability points, let's take a look at the stats more in depth.

## Base Stats

- **FT.**: Feet mmmm... Basically your movement speed, how much you can move during your turn in combat. Some races may have extra FT. dedicated to particular terrains, such as water.

- **HP** : Hit points, your health, once it hits 0 you're down and out.

- **MP** : Mana points, energy required to cast spells and some skills, can be replenished with items, some skills, and resting.

- **SP** : Soul Power, Force that starts building up within you whenever you hit an enemy with a normal attack, you get hit, or you defend. Required for most Non-Magic skills. You start battles with 10% of your max SP.

- **ATK**: Your physical Strength to deal blows, if it requires muscle, then this stat is involved. It directly determines the extra damage you deal with weapons that scale with it, and you're proficient with.

- **DEF**: Your Sturdiness to physical blows, how much of a beatdown you can take without flinching. It is used by the DM when determining the amount of damage you take, if your defence is high enough, you can shrug off weak attacks.

- **MAG:** Your innate Magical power. It directly determines the extra damage you deal with Spells (White magic being the exception) and weapons that scale with it, and you're proficient with.

- **WIL** : Willpower, how capable you are against magic attacks, also doubling as the source of white and holy magic. It is used by the DM when determining the amount of magical damage you take, if your willpower is high enough, you may shrug off weaker spells. It also directly determines the extra healing and damage you deal with Spells.

- **AGI**: Your Agility, how fast you're and how good your reflexes can be are determined by this. It determines the turn order in battle, and directly determines the extra damage you deal with weapons that scale with it, and you're proficient with.
  
- **DEX**: Your Dexterity, how good your fine motor control is, whether it's dancing, precision, or... unique, kinds of attacks, dexterity is what you need. It directly determines the extra damage you deal with weapons that scale with it, and you're proficient with.


Now that we've seen them, let me explain how you'll roll your character's stats.

## Rolling your Stats

### Movement Speed

Movement Speed (Ft.), is calculated differently from the other base stats, since it is directly correlated to agility.

Every character starts with 25Ft. and then, if the agility stat is beyond certain thresholds, the character may gain or lose extra Movement Speed. I'll leave a table illustrating the first stages of thresholds thus far. **If the agility stat is increased mid battle via a skill, it will not count towards this. Ft. only increase or decrease during a long rest or Job|Race change**

### Others

For every other Base stat (Including HP, MP, and SP) **you'll have to roll 5d6, remove the lowest number, and then add up the rest to get the result**, I strongly recommend doing grouped rolls, so you can then choose to distribute the results across the stats as you wish, rather than rolling for one stat, then the other, and so on.

Example:

|          | 1st Set     | 2nd set     | 3rd set     | 4th set     | 5th set     | 6th set     | 7th set     | 8th set     | 9th set     |
| -------- | ----------- | ----------- | ----------- | ----------- | ----------- | ----------- | ----------- | ----------- | ----------- |
| 1st roll | 6           | 2 (Ignored) | 3 (Ignored) | 6           | 4           | 4           | 1 (Ignored) | 5           | 1 (Ignored) |
| 2nd roll | 1 (Ignored) | 6           | 6           | 6           | 4           | 4           | 2           | 6           | 1           |

| 4th roll | 6           | 4           | 5           | 4           | 5           | 5           | 4           | 2           | 4           |
| 5th roll | 2           | 3           | 6           | 1 (Ignored) | 1 (Ignored) | 2 (Ignored) | 6           | 5           | 4           |
| Total    | 20          | 16          | 21          | 20          | 18          | 19          | 15          | 18          | 10          |

One could then distribute the values as they pleased between the Base Stats

## Ability Points

In order to make multi-classing worth it, Abilities and ability points were introduced.

For the most part, if you were to master a job like martial artist, and then switch to magician, you'd lose all proficiencies that you had before, however, alongside normal skills that you learn, levelling up job also teaches you abilities.

These abilities remain permanently unlocked for your character once you learn them, however they won't take effect unless you equip them, you do this by expending one or more ability points, depending on what it does.

It should be noted that Abilities of the same name do not stack, for example, if you were to learn Agility +10% on one job, and then reach a level on another job that would unlock it, nothing would happen. In the same vein, You may not stack an ability like Agility +10% with another called Agility +20%, you'd have to decide between the two.

These points can be allocated or refunded when taking long rests, or when visiting a place that allows you to change your job / race.

To make an example, the Martial Artist job learns an ability that increases the crit rate of your attacks, normally this pairs up nicely with its passive, however other classes can benefit from said crit increase as well, such as a normal warrior who might want to be able to nail more crits while wielding a sword he's proficient with.

The number of ability points a player is set like this

**1 + 1d4 + [[Level System#Personal Level | Personal Level]]**

Meaning at character creation you roll the 1d4, and then add 1 every time you level up.
## Secondary Stats

While these statistics are not rolled for, they do have a strong effect, and because of it, they are linked to Job/Races Passives, Status effects, and Abilities.

- **Counter Rate**: As the term implies, how likely are you to be able to react to a PHYSICAL attack, it is measured in percentages and is tracked by the DM to check against it. The nature of the counter, by default, is a regular attack with whatever weapon you have equipped.  **Counters do not stop the damage from the attack**. Starts at 0%, Caps at 100%

- **Critical Rate**: Instead of aiming for a specific Roll, PHYSICAL critical hits are determined by this, it is measured in percentages and is tracked by the DM to check against it. Scoring a critical hit makes it so the damage is doubled for that particular strike (Meaning multi-strike skills would have a Crit check on each hit. Starts at 0%, Caps at 100%

- **Critical Damage**: The modifier that defines how much damage a critical hit inflicts, it is measured in percentages and it is automatically used to modify the damage of ANY hit that is a crit. Starts at 200% (Double Damage), has no Cap

- **Magic Critical Rate**: Instead of aiming for a specific Roll, MAGIC critical hits are determined by this, it is measured in percentages and is tracked by the DM to check against it. Scoring a critical hit makes it so the damage is doubled for that particular strike (Meaning multi-strike skills would have a Crit check on each hit. Starts at 0%, Caps at 100%

- **Evasion**: Used to determine whether or not you dodge out of the way from a PHYSICAL attack, effectively taking no damage. The Attack roll is compared against this to check the outcome. Measured in percentages. Starts at 1%, caps at 100% (possible but you'd have to work your ass off to get it)

- **Magic Evasion**: Used to determine whether or not you dodge out of the way from a MAGICAL attack, effectively taking no damage. The Attack roll is compared against this to check the outcome. Measured in percentages. Starts at 1%, caps at 100% (possible but you'd have to work your ass off to get it)

- **Auto-Hit Evasion**: Used to determine whether or not you dodge out of the way from an Auto-Hit Skill attack, effectively taking no damage. The Attack roll is compared against this to check the outcome. Measured in percentages. Starts at 0%, caps at 33% (Something needs to be absolute)

- **Reflect Rate**: Used to determine whether or not you reflect a PHYSICAL attack back to the enemy, effectively taking no damage while damaging the enemy. The attack roll is compared against this to check the outcome. Measured in percentages. Starts at 0% caps at 40%

- **Magic Reflect Rate**: Used to determine whether or not you reflect a MAGICAL attack back to the enemy, effectively taking no damage while damaging the enemy. The attack roll is compared against this to check the outcome. Measured in percentages. Starts at 0% caps at 40%

- **Hit Rate**: Value added to attack rolls, increasing or decreasing the likelihood of a strike landing. Measured in percentages, each % equals a +1, meaning a 5% increase means a +5 on the attack rolls. Starts at 0%, has no cap. **This value can be negative.**
