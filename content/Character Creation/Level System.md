
Players will have to choose a Basic Race and a Basic Job to assign to their character, and when starting the campaign, they will be set at level 1.

Characters have 3 levels:

- A Job Level
- A Race Level
- Personal Level

### Job and Race levels

Both the Race and Job Level work in a similar fashion, you get XP from battles and/or performing tasks that involve or are otherwise related to your profession / race. For example, if you're a cook, and you challenge yourself to make a complicated dish, you'll get some job XP. (You do not however, gain job XP for the cook job if you're currently a on different job.)

A racial example would be, if you're a slime, and you're trying to sneak around, you take it upon yourself to shape your body in a way that would not attract attention, as a big BIG puddle, there you'd gain Race XP.

Basic and Intermediate Jobs/Races cap at level 10, to see what you learn in each level of your specific combination please refer to [[Job - Race System]].

### Personal Level

Since you're able and encouraged to switch between Jobs (and Races once you're able) a personal level disconnected from the former ones is necessary.

You gain XP for this solely by winning battles (Partial victories, such as beating some enemies, and then fleeing count as well), and on level up, while you do not learn any skills, your overall base stats increase.

So the personal level is present entirely to make an overarching stat progression that allows for players to switch and test around jobs as they please, without sacrificing their entire strength and investment.
